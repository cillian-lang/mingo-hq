How It Works

Sign up with your email, create your event (name, date, venue, ticket types, pricing), and publish. Tickets are live immediately. Takes about 5 minutes.

No. MINGO is a progressive web app (PWA). It works in any mobile browser on any phone. No app store, no download, no friction.

Security & Fraud
How does MINGO prevent fake tickets? +

Every ticket is minted as an NFT (non-fungible token) on the Hedera network. This means each ticket is cryptographically unique and verifiable. It literally cannot be duplicated or faked. When scanned at the door, the system checks the blockchain in real-time.

What about scalping? +

You set the rules. MINGO lets you cap resale prices ‚Äî for example, no more than 110% of face value. This is enforced at the smart contract level, meaning it can't be bypassed. Fans pay fair prices.

Can tickets be transferred or resold? +

Yes, through the MINGO platform. You control whether resale is allowed, and at what price cap. If a ticket is resold, you (the organiser) automatically receive a royalty on the resale. Every time.

What if someone screenshots another person's QR code? +

Won't work. Each ticket is tied to a specific account and verified on-chain. A screenshot doesn't transfer ownership. The scanner will reject it.

Resale & Royalties
How do resale royalties work? +

When you create an event, you set a resale royalty percentage. If a ticket is resold through MINGO, that percentage automatically goes to you. This is enforced by smart contract ‚Äî no manual tracking, no honour system.

What's a typical royalty percentage? +

That's up to you. Many organisers set 5-15% of the resale price. For the first time, you earn from secondary market activity instead of losing it entirely.

Is resale only through MINGO? +

Resale is managed through the MINGO platform to ensure price caps and royalties are enforced. Tickets can't be resold on external secondary marketplaces (which is a feature, not a bug ‚Äî it's how we prevent scalping).

Technology
What is Hedera? +

Hedera is a public distributed ledger (similar to blockchain) governed by major global enterprises including Google, IBM, Boeing, Deutsche Telekom, and LG. It processes over 10,000 transactions per second with sub-second finality. It's carbon negative. It's the infrastructure that powers MINGO's ticket verification.
